# Brand Identity Chamber

The Brand Identity Chamber is where you define who your brand is. The information you set here is fed directly into every AI generation — it shapes the tone of copy, the style of captions, and the creative direction of all output.

**Access:** Tools Panel → Vault Generator (then tap Brand Chamber) or Settings → Brands → Edit Brand

---

## Why Brand Identity Matters

Without a configured Brand Identity, the AI generates generic content. With a fully configured brand, the AI knows:

- What your brand sounds like (formal vs casual, serious vs playful)
- What your brand looks like (colours, visual palette)
- Who your brand is speaking to (see [Audience Arena](./04-audience-arena.md))

The difference between a generic caption and a caption that feels authentically on-brand is almost entirely determined by the quality of your Brand Identity setup.

## Setting Up Your Brand

### Brand Name
Your brand name is set when you create the brand. To rename a brand: **Settings → Brands → [Brand Name] → Rename**.

### Logo Upload

Tap the logo upload area and select an image from your device. Supported formats: PNG, JPG, SVG. Maximum size: 5MB.

Your logo is used as a reference for:
- Visual identity context in AI prompts
- Display in the Workspace Header brand switcher
- Generated content briefs that reference the brand visually

> **Best practice:** Upload a clean, high-contrast version of your logo on a transparent or white background for the best AI context.

### Brand Colours

Select up to 5 colours that represent your brand palette. Tap the colour orbs to toggle them on or off.

The default palette offers 6 preset colours. To use a custom colour:
1. Tap **+ Custom colour**
2. Enter a hex code (e.g. `#FF6B35`) or use the colour picker
3. Tap **Add**

Brand colours inform the AI about the visual energy of your brand. A brand with deep navy and gold signals premium/formal. A brand with coral and lime signals playful/energetic.

### Brand Tone

The tone setting is the most important variable for AI output quality. Select the tone that best describes how your brand communicates.

| Tone | What it means | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Professional** | Formal, authoritative, polished language | B2B, finance, legal, consulting |
| **Casual** | Conversational, relaxed, approachable | Lifestyle brands, consumer apps |
| **Humorous** | Witty, playful, entertainment-first | Food and beverage, entertainment, youth brands |
| **Inspirational** | Motivational, aspirational, emotionally driven | Fitness, wellness, personal development |
| **Authoritative** | Expert, research-backed, confident | Healthcare, education, tech |
| **Playful** | Colourful, energetic, fun | Children's products, games, creative tools |

> **Can't decide?** Think about the brand you aspire to be, not just where you are today. The tone should feel slightly aspirational.

## Managing Multiple Brands

Marketer Pro Office supports multiple brands on all plans.

| Plan | Brand limit |
|---|---|
| Free | 1 brand |
| Pro | Up to 5 brands |
| Enterprise | Unlimited brands |

### Creating a New Brand
Tap the brand switcher in the Workspace Header → **+ New Brand**

### Switching Between Brands
Tap the brand name in the Workspace Header → select the brand you want to work on. The entire Desk updates to show that brand's tasks, calendar, and settings.

### Deleting a Brand
**Settings → Brands → [Brand Name] → Delete Brand**

> ⚠️ Deleting a brand permanently removes all associated tasks, scheduled posts, and artifacts. This cannot be undone.

---

## Re-entering the Brand Identity Chamber

You can return to the Brand Identity Chamber at any time to update your logo, colours, or tone. Your changes take effect on the next generation.

To re-enter: **Settings → Brands → [Brand Name] → Open Identity Chamber**

Each time you complete the Brand Identity Chamber, you earn **50 XP**.

---

## Tips

- **Update your tone seasonally.** A brand running a summer promotion might want a more playful, casual tone even if their year-round voice is professional.
- **Use multiple brands for different campaigns.** Some users create a separate "brand" for a specific product launch to keep the AI focused on that campaign's identity.
- **Colours matter more than you think.** The AI uses colour descriptors (warm, cool, vibrant, muted) to influence creative direction. Don't skip this step.
