# Audience Arena

The Audience Arena is where you define who your brand is speaking to. Audience traits are fed directly into the AI generation engine alongside your brand identity, dramatically improving the relevance and targeting of generated content.

**Access:** Tools Panel → Audiences

---

## What Are Audience Traits?

Audience traits are characteristics that describe your ideal customer or follower. They are organised into three categories:

| Category | What it covers | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Demographic** | Age and life stage | 18–24, 25–34, 35–44, 45+ |
| **Psychographic** | Values, mindset, identity | Entrepreneur, Creative, Tech-savvy, Budget-conscious |
| **Behavioural** | How they act and buy | Mobile-first, Impulse buyer, Researcher, Brand loyalist |

You can mix traits across all three categories. The AI uses the combination to understand not just who your audience is, but how they think and behave.

---

## Collecting Traits

Traits in the Audience Arena are represented as collectible items — like unlocking intel on your target market.

1. Open the Audience Arena from the Tools Panel
2. Tap any trait tile to collect it — it appears in the collected row at the top
3. Tap a collected trait chip to remove it
4. Collect as many traits as accurately describe your audience
5. Tap **Lock Audience Intel** to save

> **There is no minimum or maximum.** Collect as many traits as accurately represent your audience. More specificity gives the AI better context, but too many contradictory traits can dilute the focus.

---

## Trait Combinations That Work Well

**Direct-to-consumer lifestyle brand targeting young professionals:**
- 25–34 + Creative + Entrepreneur + Mobile-first + Impulse buyer

**B2B SaaS tool for small business owners:**
- 35–44 + Entrepreneur + Tech-savvy + Researcher + Budget-conscious

**Fitness and wellness brand:**
- 25–34 + Inspirational + Mobile-first + Brand loyalist

**Budget consumer goods brand:**
- 18–24 + Budget-conscious + Mobile-first + Impulse buyer

---

## How Traits Affect AI Output

When you fire the Vault Generator with an audience defined, the AI adjusts:

- **Vocabulary and register** — Tech-savvy audiences get more specific language; Budget-conscious audiences get value-focused copy
- **Platform strategy** — Mobile-first audiences get content optimised for vertical formats and thumb-stopping hooks
- **Emotional appeal** — Entrepreneur traits shift copy towards ambition, growth, and efficiency; Loyalist traits shift towards community and belonging
- **Call to action style** — Impulse buyers get urgency-driven CTAs ("Only 24 hours left") while Researchers get evidence-driven CTAs ("See the full comparison")

## Managing Multiple Audiences

You can create multiple audience profiles for a single brand — useful if your brand speaks to distinct customer segments.

1. From the Tools Panel → Audiences, tap **+ New Audience**
2. Give it a name (e.g. "Young Professionals", "Small Business Owners")
3. Collect traits and lock the intel

When using the Generator Form, you can select which audience profile to use for that specific generation.

---

## Updating an Audience

To edit an existing audience: **Tools Panel → Audiences → [Audience Name] → Edit**

Changes take effect on the next generation. Updating your audience does not affect previously generated artifacts.

---

## Earning XP

Completing the Audience Arena for the first time awards **50 XP**. Subsequent audience updates do not award XP, but creating a new audience profile awards **25 XP**.

---

## Tips

- **Be honest, not aspirational.** Define your actual current audience, not who you wish your audience was. The AI generates better content when it's calibrated to reality.
- **Update seasonally.** If you run a promotion aimed at a different segment, create a new audience profile for that campaign and switch to it when generating.
- **Don't over-collect.** Collecting conflicting traits (e.g. both Impulse buyer and Researcher) can create mixed signals. Stick to the dominant traits for your primary audience.
